Biography

Kharis Yonatan (born 1994, Sukabumi, West Java, Indonesia) is a self-made investor, freelance SEO practitioner, and independent thinker based in Southeast Asia. The name Kharis is derived from the Greek word meaning "grace" — a name his parents chose after years of waiting for a child they feared would never come.

Early Life

Born to Tan Kim Jauw (Rudi) and Lim Tjeh (Rita) — both of whom married above the age of 33, after years of difficulty conceiving. He attended TKK Ketilang Sukabumi, later acquired by BPK Penabur, then continued at SDK BPK Penabur Sukabumi. An early love for mathematics quietly dissolved after a strict teacher in third grade. The love for numbers, however, never entirely left him — it would resurface decades later in a different form.

Basketball & The First Departure

Introduced to basketball by a physical education teacher, what began as recreation became discipline. By grade five and six, he was competing regionally, helping his school place fourth in the BPK Penabur national sports competition across West Java. At SMP BPK Penabur, the team rarely finished below first. That track record earned him an invitation to SMA PSKD 1 Jakarta. He went. And he came back. The five years that followed — ages fifteen to twenty — became the quiet foundation of everything that came after.

Loss & The Rebuild

His father passed away while the family business struggled. Relatives offered criticism instead of support. At twenty-three, he found footing as a remote SEO practitioner, then enrolled in an equivalency program and was accepted to Far Eastern Bible College Singapore. His mother passed away while he was abroad. He returned to Indonesia without a degree, without a home, carrying debt from predatory lending. He returned to freelance SEO work, started investing — private bonds, equities, government instruments, physical gold. A carefully accumulated position in gold was eventually liquidated and converted into a property, owned outright. Five years. No inheritance. No safety net.

Philosophy

His intellectual formation resists easy categorization. He grew up within a Christian framework, studied theology formally in Singapore, and returned with a philosophical orientation shaped more by questions than by answers. His touchstone is Stoicism — practiced as a daily operating system, not an academic exercise. He thinks in systems. He evaluates everything through the lens of intrinsic value versus price paid. Anti-compromise as principle. Pragmatic in execution. The destination is fixed. The path is flexible.

Principles

Most people have opinions. These are conclusions — non-negotiable, not because they were declared, but because they survived long enough to be trusted.

01
Everything has a price. Know what you're paying.
Not just money — time, energy, opportunity, peace of mind. The real expensive things are never priced in currency.
02
Truth before comfort. Always.
An echo chamber is the most expensive room you can live in. The cost shows up years later, in the form of decisions that felt right at the time.
03
Risk first. Opportunity second.
Most people ask "how much can I make?" before "how much can I lose?" That's backwards. Understand the downside completely before acting on the upside.
